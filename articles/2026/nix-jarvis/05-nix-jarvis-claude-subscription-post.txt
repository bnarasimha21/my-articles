I built Nix, a personal AI assistant that is always on and remembers everything I tell it.

It answers on my Mac by voice and on my phone over Telegram. Same memory files underneath, so the conversation I start at my desk continues on the train without me having to re-explain anything.

Under the hood: a Claude Code session kept alive in tmux, a LaunchAgent for reboots, and Markdown files as long-term memory.

One long Saturday to build. Every day since, it just runs.

The Jarvis dream was never about unlimited tokens. It was about an assistant that is always there and actually knows you.
